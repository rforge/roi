Example 1

ROI allows to assign types to the variables, possible types are:

B   binary
I   integer
C   continuous
\begin{equation} \text{maximize } \ \ 3 x_1 + 1 x_2 + 3 x_3 \end{equation} \begin{eqnarray} \text{subject to } & -1 x_1 & + & 2 x_2 & + & x_3 & \leq 4 \\ & & & 4 x_2 & - & 3 x_3 & \leq 2 \\ & x_1 & - & 3 x_2 & + & 2 x_3 & \leq 3 \end{eqnarray} $$x_1, x_3 \in \mathbb{Z}_{\geq 0}$$ $$x_2 \geq 0$$ 
mat <- matrix(c(-1,  2,  1,
                 0,  4, -3, 
                 1, -3,  2), nrow = 3, byrow=TRUE)
lp <- OP(objective = c(3, 1, 3),
         constraints = L_constraint(L = mat,
                                    dir = c("<=", "<=", "<="),
                                    rhs = c(4, 2, 3)),
         types = c("I", "C", "I"),
         maximum = TRUE)

sol <- ROI_solve(lp)
sol
## Optimal solution found.
## The objective value is: 2.675000e+01
solution(sol)
## [1] 5.00 2.75 3.00

Example 2

$$\text{maximize } \ \ 3 x_1 + 1 x_2 + 3 x_3$$ $$\begin{array}{rrrrrrr} subject \ to & -1 x_1 & + & 2 x_2 & + & x_3 & \leq 4 \\ & & & 4 x_2 & - & 3 x_3 & \leq 2 \\ & x_1 & - & 3 x_2 & + & 2 x_3 & \leq 3 \end{array} $$ $$ x_1, \ x_3 \in \mathbb{Z} $$ $$ -\infty \le x_1 \leq 4 $$ $$ 0 \leq x_2 \leq 100 $$ $$ 2 \leq x_3 \le \infty $$ 
mat <- matrix(c(-1,  2,  1,
                 0,  4, -3, 
                 1, -3,  2), nrow = 3, byrow=TRUE)
bounds <- V_bound(li = c(1L, 3L), ui = c(1L, 2L),
                  lb = c(-Inf, 2), ub = c(4, 100))
lp <- OP(objective = c(3, 1, 3),
         constraints = L_constraint(L = mat,
                                    dir = c("<=", "<=", "<="),
                                    rhs = c(4, 2, 3)),
         types = c("I", "C", "I"),
         bounds = bounds,
         maximum = TRUE)
sol <- ROI_solve(lp)
sol
## Optimal solution found.
## The objective value is: 2.350000e+01
solution(sol)
## [1] 4.0 2.5 3.0
