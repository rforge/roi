Linear Programming with ROI

Example 1

Shows how ROI can be used to solve a linear problem.

library("ROI")
## ROI: R Optimization Infrastructure
## Registered solver plugins: nlminb, ecos, glpk, ipop, quadprog, scs, symphony.
## Default solver: auto.
$$\text{maximize } \ \ 2 x_1 + 4 x_2 + 3 x_3$$ $$\begin{array}{rrrrrrr} subject \ to & 3 x_1 & + & 4 x_2 & + & 2 x_3 & \leq 60 \\ & 2 x_1 & + & x_2 & + & 2 x_3 & \leq 40 \\ & x_1 & + & 3 x_2 & + & 2 x_3 & \leq 80 \end{array} $$ $$x_1, x_2, x_3 \geq 0$$ 
mat <- matrix(c(3, 4, 2,
                2, 1, 2,
                1, 3, 2), nrow=3, byrow=TRUE)
lp <- OP(objective = c(2, 4, 3),
         constraints = L_constraint(L = mat,
                                    dir = c("<=", "<=", "<="),
                                    rhs = c(60, 40, 80)),
         maximum = TRUE)

List the applicable solvers.

ROI_applicable_solvers(lp)
## [1] "nlminb"   "ecos"     "glpk"     "ipop"     "scs"   "symphony"

Solve the problem.

sol <- ROI_solve(lp, solver = "glpk")
sol
## Optimal solution found.
## The objective value is: 7.666667e+01
